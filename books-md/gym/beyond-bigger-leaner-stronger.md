## Core training principles to wire into the Gym/Build pillar
- **Progressive overload is the #1 driver.** Muscle grows when you force it to lift more over time. Track your lifts; aim to add reps or weight. The app's streaks should reward *getting stronger*, not just showing up.
- **Heavy compound lifts** (squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press) do the heavy lifting for size and strength. Prioritize them over isolation/machines.
- **Periodization & deloads.** You can't push max intensity forever. Planned lighter weeks prevent plateaus and injury — rest is part of the program, not a failure.
- **Train in moderate-heavy rep ranges** (≈4–6 to 8–10 reps) with real intensity. Effort and proximity to failure matter more than chasing the "pump."
- **Recovery = where growth happens.** Sleep, protein, and not overtraining. More is not always better.

## Nutrition (Clean Food pillar)
- **Energy balance rules body weight:** a calorie deficit cuts fat, a slight surplus builds muscle. You can't out-train a bad diet.
- **Protein is king for building muscle** — roughly 0.8–1g per pound of bodyweight per day. Spread across meals.
- **Flexible dieting / macros** beat rigid "clean vs. dirty" thinking — hit your protein and calorie targets consistently and you progress.
- **Consistency over perfection.** One bad meal doesn't matter; ten weeks of adherence does. (Ties straight into the streak mechanic.)
