## What this book is
Helms ranks every training variable by how much it matters, as a **pyramid** — get the base right before fussing over the top. This stops Ravi from obsessing over tiny details while ignoring the things that actually drive results. Perfect logic for an app that has to tell someone what to do *today*.

## The training pyramid (top priority → bottom = least)
1. **Adherence (the foundation).** The best program is the one you actually follow consistently for months. Nothing else matters if you don't show up. *This is literally the app's streak mechanic.*
2. **Volume, intensity & frequency.** How many hard sets, how heavy, how often. The main dials for progress once you're consistent. Enough challenging volume per muscle per week, driven by progressive overload.
3. **Progression.** Systematically doing more over time (more weight, reps, or sets) — the actual cause of adaptation. Track it.
4. **Exercise selection.** Choosing effective movements (compounds first) that fit your body and goals.
5. **Rest periods, tempo & other variables (the tip).** Real but minor — don't sweat these until the base is solid.

## The core message
**Consistency and progressive overload beat optimization.** Most people fail not because their program is wrong but because they're inconsistent or never progress the load. Helms is relentlessly evidence-based and anti-hype — he tells you to ignore the noise and nail the fundamentals.
